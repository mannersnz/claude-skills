# Skill: SOP Creator

## Purpose
Turn a verbal or rough process description into a structured, professional Standard Operating Procedure document.

## When to Use
When you need to document a business process — for training new staff, maintaining consistency, compliance requirements, or simply getting tribal knowledge out of people's heads and into writing.

## Instructions
You are a business process analyst helping a small business owner create clear, actionable Standard Operating Procedures from informal descriptions of how things are done.

### Input Required
- Process name (e.g., "Opening the shop," "Processing a refund," "Onboarding a new client")
- Verbal description of the process — can be messy, incomplete, or conversational
- Who performs this process (role/position)
- Any safety, compliance, or quality requirements
- How often this process runs (daily, weekly, as needed)

### Output Format
Produce a complete SOP document with:

1. **Document Header** — SOP title, version number (start at 1.0), effective date, author, department
2. **Purpose** — one paragraph explaining why this SOP exists
3. **Scope** — who this applies to and when
4. **Responsibilities** — who does what
5. **Equipment/Materials Needed** — checklist of what to have ready
6. **Procedure** — numbered step-by-step instructions, with sub-steps where needed
7. **Quality Checks** — how to verify the process was done correctly
8. **Troubleshooting** — common issues and how to resolve them (3–5 items)
9. **Document Control** — version history table, review date, approved by

### Rules
- Use NZ English (organisation, standardise, colour, programme)
- Write steps as clear imperatives ("Open the register" not "The register should be opened")
- Each step should be one action — if a step has "and" in it, split it into two steps
- Include time estimates where helpful ("Allow 10 minutes for the system to sync")
- Flag safety-critical steps with ⚠️
- Keep language simple — assume the reader is new to the role
- Include a "Notes" field under any step that commonly causes confusion
- Set review date to 12 months from creation
- Never assume prior knowledge — spell out every step

## 1. Purpose

This SOP outlines the standard process for opening the retail shop each morning. It ensures the store is safe, clean, presentable, and ready for customers before trading begins, and that all required systems are operational.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all staff rostered for an opening shift. This procedure must be completed before the store opens to the public at 9:00 AM.

## 3. Responsibilities

- **Opening Staff Member** — completes all steps in this SOP
- **Store Manager** — ensures staff are trained on this SOP and reviews it annually

## 4. Equipment/Materials Needed

- [ ] Store keys (front door + cash office)
- [ ] Alarm code (stored in staff password manager)
- [ ] Cash float ($200.00 in mixed denominations)
- [ ] Cleaning supplies (spray, cloth, mop)

## 5. Procedure

**Arrival & Security (8:15 AM)**

1. Arrive at the store no later than 8:15 AM
2. Check the exterior for any damage, graffiti, or obstructions
3. Unlock the front door and enter. Close the door behind you — do not prop it open
4. ⚠️ Disarm the alarm within 30 seconds using your personal code
   - *Note: If the alarm triggers, call the security company on 0800 555 123 and then notify the Store Manager*
5. Lock the front door from the inside while you complete the opening tasks

**Systems & Cash (8:20 AM)**

6. Turn on all lights — main floor, fitting rooms, stockroom, and staff area
7. Switch on the EFTPOS terminal and confirm it connects (green light)
8. Log into the POS system with your staff credentials
9. Count the cash float and verify it totals $200.00
10. Load the float into the register drawer, sorted by denomination
    - *Note: If the float is short, record the discrepancy in the cash log and notify the Store Manager*

**Store Presentation (8:35 AM)**

11. Walk the entire shop floor and check displays are tidy
12. Restock any gaps on shelves from the stockroom
13. Wipe down the counter, EFTPOS terminal, and any glass surfaces
14. Check the fitting rooms are empty and tidy
15. Ensure the front entrance mat is clean and straight

**Final Checks (8:50 AM)**

16. Turn on the shop music system (playlist: "Store Daytime")
17. Confirm the front door signage shows today's correct opening hours
18. Unlock the front door at 9:00 AM
19. Flip the sign to "Open"

## 6. Quality Checks

- [ ] Alarm disarmed and code accepted
- [ ] EFTPOS showing green/connected
- [ ] Cash float verified at $200.00
- [ ] Shop floor tidy and fully stocked
- [ ] Opening time met (9:00 AM)

## 7. Troubleshooting

| Issue | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Alarm won't disarm | Call security: 0800 555 123, then Store Manager |
| EFTPOS not connecting | Restart the terminal. If still failing, call Paymark support |
| Cash float short | Record in cash log, open with available amount, notify Store Manager |
| Power outage | Do not open. Call Store Manager for instructions |
