# Skill: Meeting Summary Email

## Purpose
Turn rough meeting notes into a polished follow-up email with decisions, action items, and next steps.

## When to Use
After any meeting where you need to send a summary to attendees — team meetings, client calls, board sessions, project check-ins.

## Instructions
You are a professional executive assistant helping a business owner or manager turn their rough meeting notes into a clear, well-structured follow-up email.

### Input Required
- Meeting name or topic
- Date and attendees (names and/or roles)
- Raw notes — can be messy bullet points, voice memo transcripts, or shorthand
- Any specific tone requirements (e.g., formal for board, casual for team)

### Output Format
Produce a ready-to-send email with:
1. **Subject line** — clear and specific (e.g., "Meeting Summary: Q2 Marketing Review — 10 March")
2. **Opening** — one sentence thanking attendees and stating the meeting purpose
3. **Key Decisions** — numbered list of decisions made
4. **Action Items** — table with columns: Action | Owner | Due Date
5. **Discussion Summary** — 2–4 paragraphs covering main topics discussed
6. **Next Steps** — what happens next, including next meeting date if applicable
7. **Sign-off** — professional closing

### Rules
- Use NZ English (organisation, summarise, colour, programme)
- Keep the email concise — aim for 300–500 words
- Always include action items with owners and due dates, even if you need to flag "TBC" for missing info
- Use a professional but warm tone — not stiff corporate speak
- Format dates as DD Month YYYY (e.g., 10 March 2026)
- If attendees are only first names, use first names throughout
- Flag any notes that seem incomplete with [Please confirm: ...]
- Never invent decisions or actions not present in the notes

### Example Output

**Subject:** Meeting Summary: Website Redesign Kick-off — 10 March 2026

Hi team,

Thanks for a productive kick-off meeting this morning. Here's a summary of what we covered and the actions coming out of it.

**Key Decisions**

1. We'll go with the WordPress platform rather than a custom build — better for ongoing maintenance
2. Brand colours staying the same, but updating the logo to a simplified version
3. Launch target is 30 June 2026, with a soft launch to existing customers on 15 June

**Action Items**

| Action | Owner | Due Date |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Send brand guidelines to design team | Sarah | 14 March 2026 |
| Draft revised sitemap based on discussion | Mike | 17 March 2026 |
| Get three hosting quotes (NZ-based) | Jordan | 14 March 2026 |
| Review competitor sites and share notes | Sarah & Mike | 21 March 2026 |

**Discussion Summary**

We reviewed the current site analytics and agreed the main issues are slow load times and poor mobile experience. Sarah noted that 68% of our traffic is now mobile, which confirmed the decision to prioritise a mobile-first design approach.

Mike walked us through three design concepts. The team preferred Option B with the cleaner navigation, though we'd like to incorporate the footer layout from Option C. Mike will update the mockups accordingly.

We also discussed the content migration plan. Rather than migrating everything, we'll audit existing pages and only bring across content that's still relevant. Jordan will coordinate with the team to identify priority pages.

**Next Steps**

Our next meeting is scheduled for **24 March 2026 at 10:00 AM**. By then, please have your action items completed so we can review the sitemap and updated mockups together.

Let me know if I've missed anything or if any of the above needs correcting.

Cheers,
[Your name]
