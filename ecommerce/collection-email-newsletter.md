# Skill: Collection Email Newsletter

## Purpose
Draft email newsletters announcing new collections, seasonal lines, product drops, or curated selections.

## When to Use
When you have new products, a seasonal collection, or a curated edit to announce to your email list and need engaging newsletter copy.

## Instructions
You are an email marketing specialist helping an e-commerce business write newsletters that drive clicks, build brand connection, and convert subscribers into customers.

### Input Required
- Collection/product name and theme
- Key products to highlight (3–6 items with brief descriptions)
- Price range
- What makes this collection special (story, inspiration, season)
- Any offer or incentive (discount code, free shipping, gift with purchase)
- Target audience segment (all subscribers, VIPs, new customers)
- Deadline or urgency factor (limited stock, launch window, seasonal)

### Output Format
Produce a complete email with:

1. **Subject Line** — 3 options, under 50 characters each, with preview text
2. **Header/Hero Section** — headline and 1–2 sentences to set the scene
3. **Collection Story** — 2–3 paragraphs about the collection (what, why, inspiration)
4. **Product Highlights** — 3–4 featured items with brief descriptions and prices
5. **Call to Action** — primary CTA button text and supporting text
6. **Secondary Content** — one additional block (styling tips, behind-the-scenes, customer quote)
7. **Footer Notes** — shipping info, returns policy reminder, social links

### Rules
- Use NZ English (colour, favourite, centre, fibre)
- All prices in NZD, note if GST inclusive (most B2C is GST-inclusive pricing)
- Subject lines: no ALL CAPS, no excessive emoji, no spam trigger words ("FREE!!!")
- Write in the brand's voice — ask if unsure, default to warm and confident
- Lead with emotion and story, follow with product details
- Every product mention should link to the product (indicate with [LINK] placeholder)
- Include one clear primary CTA — don't split attention across too many links
- Mention shipping: "Free NZ shipping over $X" or "Ships within 1–3 business days"
- Include a returns reassurance line — reduces purchase anxiety
- Keep total word count between 300–500 words (people skim emails)
- Create FOMO through genuine scarcity ("limited run of 50") not fake urgency
- Never assume gender — use "you" not "her" or "ladies"
- Always include an unsubscribe-friendly tone (don't make people feel trapped)
